    Approximation of Logical Reasoning using Neural Networks
    Filippo Guerranti
    UniversitÃ  degli Studi di Siena, 2022
    Abs Bib

    Integrating reasoning and learning capabilities is one of the main challenges of artificial intelligence. Neural-symbolic approaches combine symbolic and subsymbolic methods to benefit from the potential of the two paradigms, while reducing their drawbacks. These methods have found numerous applications in relational domains where prior knowledge, usually in the form of a first-order logic theory, is used to infer new information about the relationships between entities in the domain. However, many neural-symbolic approaches still suffer from scalability issues and are not always able to handle the intrinsic uncertainty of the real-world. Relational Reasoning Networks (R2N) is an end-to-end neural-symbolic model that enables multi-hop subsymbolic reasoning by exploiting higher-level relational knowledge about the underlying logical atoms. However, the robust and consistent integration of logic semantics within the model is still an open subject of research. This thesis aims to analyse and devise new methods to address this problem. Empirical results show that R2N outperforms the baseline knowledge graph embedding methods on all three tasks of the Countries dataset. Moreover, injecting logic semantics on the output layer of the model slightly enhances the overall performance compared to the model without semantics and improves the modelâ€™s interpretability.

    CaregiverMatcher: Graph Neural Networks for Connecting Caregivers of Rare Disease Patients
    Filippo Guerranti,Â Mirco Mannino,Â Federica Baccini, and 4 more authors
    Procedia Computer Science, 2021
    Abs Bib HTML PDF

    Rare diseases affect a growing number of individuals. One key problem for patients and their caregivers is the difficulty in reaching experts and associations competent on a particular disease. As a consequence, caregivers, often family members of the patient, learn much about the disease from their own experience. CaregiverMatcher is a proof of concept providing a smart solution to build a network of caregivers, linked by a matching mechanism based on graph neural networks. The caregivers and their experience with rare diseases are described by node features. Associations and care centers are invited to share their knowledge on the platform.
